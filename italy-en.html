San Marino is definitely a historic city, so I was surprised that it took my breath always, because history isn't my cup of tea.

Walling through small alleys full of little interesting shops, meanwhile listening to Italian and breathtaking view from the top of the city? Idyllic.

Art can be found here easily as well, you just have to look a little closer.When it comes to sights, must seen is Palazzo Pubblico which is an official Government Building where official State ceremonies take place. It's also the seat of the Republic's main institutional and administrative bodies and it's located on a little piazza. And right next to Palazzo Pubblico is pizzeria called Laminona where they make delicious pizza, vegan friendly.Furthermore, there are the Three Towers of San Marino which are located on the three peaks of Monte Titano.

The oldest and the most famous one is La Guaita on the summit La Rocca (751 m) which was constructed in 11th century in order to protect the city from enemies. It served briefly as a prison as well. The second one is La Cesta on the summit La Franta (756 m) - the highest of Monte Titano's summits. A museum to honour Saint Marinus is located in this tower and showcases over 1 550 weapons dating from the Medieval Era to modern day. The last one is Il Montale on the summit La Montale (738 m), the only one which is not open to the public.Another beautiful building is Basilica di San Marino, neoclassical Catholic church built in 19th century. It is dedicated to Saint Marinus, the founder and patron of the Republic.

I can honestly say that San Marino is worth visiting. Whether you'll decide to see all the sights or just take a walk through alleys, you won't regret it. It will take your breath out with its uniqueness, impressive views and interesting history.There's something for everyone.I hope you liked my first article and that I awaken an interest in San Marino in you. Let us know if you'll visit this city for sure. Have a nice day.
